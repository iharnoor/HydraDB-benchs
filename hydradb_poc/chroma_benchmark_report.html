Evaluation Methodology

  1. Retrieve: Top-10 chunks from each system for every test question
  2. Generate: Gemini 3 Flash produces a one-sentence answer based ONLY on the top-5 retrieved chunks
  3. Judge: Gemini 3 Flash evaluates if the AI answer contains the key facts from the expected answer — YES/NO verdict + 1–10 score
  4. Winner: Determined by judge score; ties when both score equally

Key Takeaways

ChromaDB leads on answer quality; HydraDB shows graph-powered advantages in specific categories

ChromaDB's pure cosine similarity consistently surfaces the most relevant chunk at the top of results, leading to a higher average judge score (6.68 vs 5.68) and more YES verdicts (11 vs 8). However, HydraDB's hybrid retrieval shows clear advantages in multi-session reasoning (synthesizing relationship progression across multiple memories) and geographic negation (correctly filtering non-Florida trips). Both systems fail identically on temporal adjacency, chronological ordering, and geographic filtering — fundamental limitations of retrieval-based systems regardless of architecture.

ChromaDB Strength: Ranking
Simple cosine similarity puts the most relevant chunk at #1 more often, leading to better LLM answers
ChromaDB Strength: Breadth
Top-5 covers more diverse celebrations for aggregation and negation queries
HydraDB Strength: Multi-hop
Graph relations help synthesize progression across multiple related memories (6/10 vs 4/10)
Both Fail: Temporal
"Day after" and chronological ordering both score 1/10 — neither system links events by time
Both Fail: Geographic
"Outside the US" returns nothing — no geographic entity awareness in either system
10 Ties out of 19
For straightforward retrieval, both systems perform identically — the LLM compensates when the right chunk is anywhere in top-5
