The Thesis: Pure Vector Search Has Fundamental Blind Spots

Cosine similarity computes a single number: how close two embeddings are in vector space. It has no understanding of negation, time, entity relationships, or geographic structure. HydraDB's hybrid approach (vector + knowledge graph + BM25 full-text) addresses several of these gaps.

Negation Blindness
"NOT a birthday" matches birthday chunks highest
No Temporal Edges
Can't reason about "the day after" or chronological order
No Entity Direction
Can't distinguish Katie→Harnoor from Harnoor→Katie
No Geographic Awareness
"Outside the US" returns US-based events
Aggregation Ceiling
Top-k can't scan all chunks for exhaustive lists
Graph Linking
No edges between related events across memories
